External Account Transfer

Problem:

The bank did not have a way for users to transfer money from their Mutual of Omaha Bank accounts to their other bank accounts on their mobile devices.

Discovery:

  • Background:
    • The bank had completed its first major release and I was brought on to work on the version 1.1 design.
    • The original work was performed by a national agency, and the Bank decided to use the in-house design team going forward.
    • After sending out a survey to the early adopters of the mobile banking app, I put together a prioritization roadmap.
    • The most requested feature was to transfer money to external bank accounts.
  • Competitor Analysis:
    • Performing a competitor analysis on how other financial institutions did bank to bank transfers, I found that Most were very simple and able to be completed with one hand.
  • User Interviews:
    • I learned that most bank app interactions where performed while multitasking
    • Transfers were most important because our users had Mutual of Omaha Bank as a primary bank account, but they were switchers from other larger banks.
    • They decided to keep those other bank accounts open and wanted the ability to move money to them.
  • Limitations:
    • There was a major limitation on adding this feature, in that the user did not have the ability to add external banks in the mobile application. The user had to use the web portal to add and approve external banks first, then they were granted the ability to transfer out funds to other accounts. This was due to a limitation in the vended platform Mutual of Omaha Bank was using.

Wireframe:

  • This design was based as much as possible on designs that have already been implemented in the banking application in order to reduce development effort.
  • This whiteboard session included the product owner and the lead developer, which allowed us to get to a viable solution to test in just one design session.

Interactive Prototype:

Internal Validation:

  • When the prototype was complete, I shared it with the Product Owner so that they could take it to the internal stake holders for approval.
  • The external transfers prototype was approved by the internal stake holders. The second part of the internal validation phase of this solution was to introduce the new feature to the internal âdogfood panelâ. Mutual of Omaha insurance and bank employees had the ability to beta test this feature as it was in the final stages of development.
  • The internal group completed surveys weekly and did not give any negative feedback on the flow of the screens, they did however, suggest more features that they would like to see implemented in the future.

External Validation:

  • The bank has a few branches locally so I went to a two and asked 5 people to walk through the design in a live prototype from above
  • All the users were able to successfully transfer funds to an external account
